Jazz styles: swing, bossa nova and lounge — what fits when

Jazz is not one sound. From lively swing to sultry bossa nova — a guide to the styles and when to reach for each.

DK
David Kleňha
Jazz singer & bandleader · 7 min read · 26 May 2026

Say the word jazz and everyone pictures something different — and that variety is its strength. As a jazz singer I adapt the jazz style to the character of the event. Let us go through the most important styles and when each one fits.

I am David Kleňha and with my trio (jazztrio.cz) I play across genres at weddings, balls and corporate events.

Swing — energy and dancing

Swing is the heart of jazz — rhythmic, joyful, made for dancing. It suits balls, evening parties and anywhere you want to fill the floor. The era of Sinatra and Glenn Miller has a charm that works for all generations.

Bossa nova — elegance and ease

Bossa nova is gentle, sultry, Brazilian. Ideal as a backdrop to a reception, a welcome drink or a summer garden party. It sets a relaxed, cultivated atmosphere perfect for conversation.

Lounge and smooth jazz — a cultivated backdrop

Lounge and smooth jazz are unobtrusive and easy to listen to. They work beautifully at corporate events, cocktail parties and anywhere the music should set the atmosphere rather than be the centre of attention.

Jazz standards — the common denominator

Across all styles stand the jazz standards — songs from the Great American Songbook that the whole world knows. They are the backbone of the repertoire and can be played as swing, as bossa nova or as a slow ballad as needed.

Which style for which event

  • Wedding — gentle standards for the ceremony, swing for the evening fun.
  • Ball — swing and dance standards for a full floor.
  • Corporate party — lounge and bossa nova at the reception, swing in the evening.
  • Celebration — a mix to match the taste of the guest of honour and guests.

Frequently asked questions

Which jazz style suits a wedding?

Gentle standards and bossa nova for the ceremony, lively swing for the evening fun. We change styles smoothly to match the mood.

What is a jazz standard?

A well-known song from the Great American Songbook that can be played as swing, bossa nova or a ballad. It forms the core of the repertoire.

Can you adapt the style to our event?

Yes, we tune the repertoire and style to the type of event, the space and your taste.
