Wedding songs: inspiration for the whole wedding day

From the first note of the entrance to the last song on the dance floor — a guide to wedding songs for every part of the day.

DK
David Kleňha
Jazz singer & bandleader · 8 min read

Choosing your wedding songs is one of the nicest parts of the preparations. Music divides the day into chapters: the mood at the ceremony is one thing, the reception another, and the evening dance floor something else entirely. As a jazz singer I have put together an overview to help you build the repertoire for the whole wedding.

I am David Kleňha and with my trio (jazztrio.cz) I play live wedding music across the country. Let us go through the day song by song.

Ceremony: gentle and heartfelt

For the ceremony, choose slow, melodic wedding songs. Jazz standards and acoustic versions of well-known ballads work well. The key moments — the bride's entrance, the signing, the recessional — each deserve their own piece. We go into detail in our article on the wedding march and aisle songs.

Reception and congratulations: a pleasant backdrop

During the reception music plays the role of a backdrop — it should create atmosphere but let guests chat. This is where a jazz duo or trio shines, with unobtrusive swing, bossa nova and light standards. Guests of all ages will appreciate a mix of familiar tunes.

Evening party: fill the dance floor

The evening brings the fun. Here you want lively wedding songs, swing, funk and dance classics for every generation. The first dance of the newlyweds is important — we have a separate article on that. For a big dance party we recommend a larger line-up, up to a quintet (jazzquintet.cz).

Tips on how to choose

  • Pick 3–5 "must-have" songs and leave the rest to the band — it reads the mood better than a playlist.
  • Think about all generations: a mix of modern hits and evergreens.
  • Don't forget the first dance and a song for the parents.
  • Tell the band what you do not want to hear.

About the author

David Kleňha is a jazz singer and bandleader based in Prague, performing across the Czech Republic and abroad. He leads ensembles from duo to quintet and specialises in live music for weddings, balls and corporate events. Learn more on his personal site davidklenha.cz.

Frequently asked questions

How many songs should I choose in advance?

Just 3–5 key ones (entrance, first dance, a favourite song). Leave the rest to the band, which responds to the guests' mood.

Will you play a song on request?

Yes, we prepare favourite songs in advance. Just send the list with enough notice.

How do I choose music for guests of different ages?

With a mix of evergreens and modern hits. Jazz and swing work across all ages and are easy to weave together with dance classics.
